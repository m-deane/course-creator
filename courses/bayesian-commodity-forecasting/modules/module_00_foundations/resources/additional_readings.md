# Additional Readings and Resources - Module 0: Foundations

## Purpose

This document provides curated resources for strengthening prerequisite knowledge. Resources are organized by topic and difficulty level.

## Probability Theory

### Essential (Start Here)

**Books:**
1. **"Statistical Rethinking" by Richard McElreath (2020)**
   - Chapters 1-3: Bayesian probability foundations
   - **Why:** Intuitive, code-based, minimal prerequisites
   - **Format:** PDF available, video lectures on YouTube
   - **Time:** 8-12 hours for relevant chapters

2. **"Think Bayes" by Allen Downey (2021)**
   - Full book on Bayesian thinking with Python
   - **Why:** Practical, hands-on, free online
   - **Link:** https://greenteapress.com/wp/think-bayes/
   - **Time:** 15-20 hours full book

**Online Courses:**
3. **Khan Academy: Probability & Statistics**
   - Free, self-paced
   - Start with "Probability" section
   - **Link:** https://www.khanacademy.org/math/statistics-probability
   - **Time:** 10-15 hours

### Intermediate

**Books:**
4. **"Probability and Statistics" by Morris DeGroot & Mark Schervish**
   - Chapters 1-5: Classical probability
   - **Why:** Rigorous but accessible, many exercises
   - **Prerequisites:** Calculus, some linear algebra

5. **"Introduction to Probability" by Dimitri Bertsekas & John Tsitsiklis**
   - MIT course textbook
   - **Why:** Clear explanations, engineering focus
   - **Companion:** MIT OpenCourseWare lectures available

### Advanced (For Deep Understanding)

**Books:**
6. **"Probability Theory: The Logic of Science" by E.T. Jaynes**
   - Philosophical foundations of probability
   - **Why:** Understanding probability as extended logic
   - **Warning:** Dense, requires mathematical maturity

7. **"Probability: Theory and Examples" by Rick Durrett**
   - Measure-theoretic probability
   - **Why:** Graduate-level rigor
   - **Prerequisites:** Real analysis

## Statistics & Inference

### Essential

**Books:**
1. **"The Lady Tasting Tea" by David Salsburg**
   - History of statistical ideas
   - **Why:** Intuitive understanding without math
   - **Time:** 6-8 hours

2. **"Naked Statistics" by Charles Wheelan**
   - Conceptual statistics
   - **Why:** Non-technical, motivating examples
   - **Time:** 8-10 hours

**Online:**
3. **StatQuest (YouTube Channel)**
   - Josh Starmer's video explanations
   - Search: "StatQuest Bayes theorem", "StatQuest distributions"
   - **Why:** Visual, intuitive, perfect for review

### Intermediate

**Books:**
4. **"All of Statistics" by Larry Wasserman**
   - Concise statistical theory
   - **Why:** Covers frequentist and Bayesian, rigorous but readable
   - **Time:** Chapter 1-6 for foundations (~10 hours)

5. **"Statistical Inference" by Casella & Berger**
   - Standard graduate textbook
   - **Why:** Comprehensive, many exercises
   - **Prerequisites:** Probability theory, calculus

## Bayesian Thinking (Pre-Course Primer)

### Conceptual Understanding

**Books:**
1. **"The Theory That Would Not Die" by Sharon Bertsch McGrayne**
   - History of Bayes' theorem
   - **Why:** Motivating stories, no math required
   - **Time:** 8-10 hours

**Articles:**
2. **"An Intuitive Explanation of Bayes' Theorem" by Eliezer Yudkowsky**
   - Online essay
   - **Link:** https://yudkowsky.net/rational/bayes
   - **Time:** 1 hour

### Practical Introduction

**Online Courses:**
3. **"Bayesian Statistics: From Concept to Data Analysis" (Coursera)**
   - UC Santa Cruz course
   - **Why:** Gentle introduction, MCMC basics
   - **Time:** 12-15 hours

**Books:**
4. **"Bayesian Data Analysis" by Gelman et al.**
   - The Bible of Bayesian statistics
   - **Chapters 1-3:** Pre-read before course starts
   - **Warning:** Dense, but worth it

## Study Plan Suggestions

### If You Have 1 Week Before Course Starts

**Priority:**
1. Khan Academy: Probability (5 hours)
2. 3Blue1Brown: Linear Algebra series (3 hours)
3. NumPy/Pandas quick tutorials (4 hours)
4. "Think Bayes" Chapters 1-3 (3 hours)

**Total:** ~15 hours, solid foundation

### If You Have 2-4 Weeks

**Week 1:**
- Statistical Rethinking Ch 1-3 (8 hours)
- Khan Academy: Probability & Statistics (10 hours)

**Week 2:**
- Linear Algebra (Khan Academy or 3Blue1Brown) (10 hours)
- Python refresher (NumPy, Pandas, Matplotlib) (8 hours)

**Week 3:**
- Hyndman & Athanasopoulos Ch 1-3 (6 hours)
- Commodity markets overview (readings) (4 hours)
- PyMC examples (6 hours)

**Week 4:**
- Review weak areas
- Complete Module 0 diagnostic

**Total:** ~50 hours, strong preparation

### If You Need Extensive Review (4+ Weeks)

Follow the 2-4 week plan, then add:
- DeGroot & Schervish: Probability chapters
- "All of Statistics" selected chapters
- "Python for Data Analysis" full book
- "Forecasting: Principles and Practice" Ch 1-6

## Learning Tips

### 1. Active Learning

**Don't just read:**
- Work through examples by hand
- Code along with tutorials
- Explain concepts aloud to yourself

### 2. Spaced Repetition

**Review schedule:**
- Immediate: After learning
- 1 day later
- 1 week later
- 1 month later

### 3. Mix Theory and Practice

**Alternate:**
- 30 min reading → 30 min coding
- Prevents burnout, reinforces concepts

### 4. Join Communities

**Get help:**
- r/statistics (Reddit)
- Cross Validated (StackExchange)
- PyMC Discourse forum
- Course discussion board

### 5. Set Concrete Goals

**Example weekly goal:**
- Complete 2 Khan Academy modules
- Code 3 NumPy examples from scratch
- Read 1 chapter of Statistical Rethinking
- Post 1 question/answer on forum
