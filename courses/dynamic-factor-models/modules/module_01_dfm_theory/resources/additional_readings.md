# Module 01: Additional Readings - Dynamic Factor Models

## Essential Papers

### 1. Stock & Watson (2002) - The Foundation
**"Forecasting Using Principal Components from a Large Number of Predictors"**
- *Journal of the American Statistical Association*, 97(460): 1167-1179
- [Link](https://www.jstor.org/stable/3085839)

**Why read:** The paper that launched modern DFM applications in economics. Clear exposition, practical methods.

---

### 2. Bai & Ng (2002) - Factor Selection
**"Determining the Number of Factors in Approximate Factor Models"**
- *Econometrica*, 70(1): 191-221

**Why read:** Information criteria for choosing number of factors. Essential for practitioners.

---

### 3. Forni et al. (2000) - Generalized DFM
**"The Generalized Dynamic Factor Model: Identification and Estimation"**
- *Review of Economics and Statistics*, 82(4): 540-554

**Why read:** Allows richer dynamic structure. Used by ECB for nowcasting.

---

### 4. Stock & Watson (2016) - Handbook Chapter
**"Dynamic Factor Models, Factor-Augmented Vector Autoregressions"**
- *Handbook of Macroeconomics*, Volume 2

**Why read:** Comprehensive survey. Connects DFMs to structural analysis.

---

## Applications

### 5. Giannone, Reichlin, & Small (2008) - Nowcasting
**"Nowcasting: The Real-Time Informational Content of Macroeconomic Data"**
- *Journal of Monetary Economics*, 55(4): 665-676

**Why read:** How central banks use DFMs for real-time GDP estimation.
