# Module 0: Foundations of Reinforcement Learning — Recommended Readings

## Essential Reading

- **Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction (2nd ed.), Chapters 1–3** — Sutton & Barto, 2018. The definitive textbook introduction to the RL problem formulation, Markov decision processes, and the exploration-exploitation tradeoff; freely available online.

- **Reinforcement Learning: A Survey** — Kaelbling, Littman & Moore, 1996. A landmark survey that establishes shared vocabulary and situates RL within the broader landscape of machine learning and control theory; essential context for understanding the field's origins.

- **An Introduction to Deep Reinforcement Learning** — Francois-Lavet, Henderson, Islam, Joelle Pineau & Precup, 2018. A compact modern survey that bridges classical RL foundations to deep learning approaches, useful for calibrating what this course builds toward.

## Supplementary Reading

- **A (Long) Peek into Reinforcement Learning** — Lilian Weng, 2018. A well-structured blog post that consolidates core RL concepts — MDPs, Bellman equations, model-free vs. model-based — with clean notation and illustrative diagrams.

- **Markov Decision Processes: Discrete Stochastic Dynamic Programming** — Puterman, 1994. The rigorous mathematical treatment of MDPs; consult Chapter 1 and Chapter 3 for the formal underpinnings behind the intuitive Sutton & Barto presentation.

- **Simple Statistical Gradient-Following Algorithms for Connectionist Reinforcement Learning** — Williams, 1992. Though primarily a policy gradient paper, Section 2 provides an unusually clear statement of the general RL problem setup that rewards careful reading even at the foundations stage.

## Online Resources

- **David Silver's UCL RL Lecture Series (Lecture 1: Introduction to Reinforcement Learning)** — Silver, DeepMind/UCL. Sixteen lectures recorded at University College London; Lecture 1 covers the MDP framework with exceptional clarity. Search "David Silver RL lectures UCL" for slides and video.

- **OpenAI Spinning Up: Key Concepts in RL** — OpenAI. A practitioner-oriented introduction covering the RL problem, policies, value functions, and the taxonomy of algorithms. Search "OpenAI Spinning Up key concepts".

- **Lilian Weng's RL blog series** — Weng, OpenAI. A multi-part series expanding on MDP foundations, value functions, and policy optimization. Search "lilianweng.github.io exploration strategies" for the full catalogue.
